Section 01
Brand Guide
01
Brand Summary
We are a neighborhood coffee shop rooted in Wellesley, MA, built on the belief that a great cup of coffee should feel like a pause, not a pit stop. We serve a community of locals who value craft, consistency, and the rare feeling of being known by name. What sets us apart isn't just what's in the cup — it's the fact that walking in feels like coming home.
02
Ideal Customer
She's 35–52, lives within a few miles, and her mornings have a lot riding on them. She's not chasing trends — she's chasing comfort and quality. She already knows what she likes and she's loyal to the places that treat her like a regular before she's earned the title. Once she finds her place, she comes back every single week.
03

Content Pillars
The Craft
The story behind how we make what we make — sourcing, technique, seasonal ingredients, the details most shops skip.
Goal: Build trust
The Morning Ritual
The feeling of a slow morning, the regulars, the light through the window, the reasons people come back.
Goal: Emotional connection
The Community
Wellesley itself, local events, the people who make up our regulars, neighborhood life.
Goal: Root us in place
The Welcome
Lowering the barrier for new customers — demystifying the menu, answering common questions, making first-timers feel ready.
Goal: Convert browsers
05
Caption Formula
Hook
One or two short lines. Set a scene, name a feeling, or say something quietly surprising. Never start with the business name.
Middle
One short paragraph. Go one layer deeper — a detail, a story, a truth. Keep it under 4 sentences.
Close
One line call to action. Make it feel like an invitation, not a command.
Example Caption
"6:47am.

This is the part of the day we don't talk about much. The prep. The quiet. The twenty minutes before everything starts.

We open at 6:30. Come for the quiet version."

07
What To Avoid
  • ✕
    Discount-forward postsYour best customers come for feeling, not deals. Promotional pricing undercuts the brand you've built.
  • ✕
    Generic celebration posts"Happy Monday!" with a stock image adds noise without adding personality.
  • ✕
    Over-explaining the menuLong ingredient lists read like a manual, not an invitation. Trust the drink to speak.
  • ✕
    Trend-chasingJumping on audio trends that have nothing to do with a slow morning in Wellesley will confuse your audience.
  • ✕
    Apologizing for posting gapsNever open a post with "we've been quiet lately." Just post. Your audience tracks feelings, not your schedule.

Here's how to build one for your business.
This dashboard was built entirely in Claude — no developers, no subscriptions, no design tools. It took two prompts and about 90 minutes. Follow the three steps below and you'll have your own version: your brand voice, your content calendar, your monthly refresh button. After that it takes about five minutes a month.

Step 1 of 3 · 15 minutes
Fill in the questionnaire
Answer all 20 questions below in plain language. Be specific — the more honest and concrete your answers, the better everything Claude produces. These answers are the foundation of your entire brand system. Question 10 is the most important: paste in something you've actually written — a caption, an email to a customer, a text. Claude will study it and mirror your voice in everything it creates.
TIP — Write in your own words. Plain language works better than polished marketing copy. You are not pitching — you are describing.
1.What does your business do, and what do you do better than anyone else?
2.Describe your best customer like a real person — age, lifestyle, what they care about, what problem they bring to you.
3.What does a customer need to feel or believe before they choose you over somewhere else?
4.If your brand were a person at a dinner party, how would they talk and carry themselves?
5.Three words you want to sound like. Three words you never want to sound like.
6.Paste your favourite thing you've ever written — a caption, an email, anything. Most important.
7.The three topics your business naturally gravitates toward.
8.What do you wish more people understood about what you do or your industry?
9.What has performed best in your content so far? What has completely flopped?
10.The one thing you want every person who sees your content to feel or think.
